# Fresh Beats: Music Streaming User & Genre Analysis

This was the first project that I worked on in the TripleTen Business Intelligence Analytics Program.  
It was a report-based project designed to showcase my ability to identify and communicate data-driven insights to stakeholders.  
The purpose was to analyze user behavior and genre performance data for Fresh Beats — a music streaming and artist promotion platform — and produce a stakeholder-ready Project Status Report addressing three core business challenges.

### Data
The data was provided by TripleTen as a Google Sheets file:
- Fresh Beats user activity and genre performance data
    - `'user type'`: free vs. paid user classification
    - `'genre'`: music genre tracked per listening session
    - `'listening activity'`: engagement volume by user type
    - `'time trends'`: genre popularity and user activity over time

### Assumptions:
- Fresh Beats generates revenue through artist promotional partnerships, making user engagement and genre visibility critical to business success.
- The provided dataset and pre-built visualizations are accurate and representative of platform-wide behavior.
- Insights selected from the data are sufficient to address all three business challenge areas.

### Findings:
1. Pop genre peaked in 2021 and has been on a downward trend since.
2. Electronic and hip hop are both on an upward trend, making them strong candidates for artist promotion investment.
3. Paid users are more active — they listen to more music than free users.
4. Paid users show steadier growth and better retention than free users, making conversion a high-value growth lever.
5. Rock is the most popular genre among free users — a key segment to target for conversion.
6. Indie is particularly popular with paid users, suggesting it as a strong genre for loyalty-building promotions.

### Recommendations:
1. Launch focused marketing campaigns targeting the demographics of electronic and hip hop fans, as these genres are growing.
2. Increase the platform's catalogue of electronic and hip hop tracks — actively solicit artists in these genres with time-limited promotional deals.
3. Conduct deeper research into why pop is declining to understand churn risk in that segment.
4. Introduce intermediate pricing tiers between free and paid to lower the conversion barrier.
5. Implement a referral marketing program offering free trial upgrades to users who bring in new listeners.
6. Design rock-focused promotions that offer free trials of the paid tier to convert the large rock-listening free user base.
7. Experiment with cross-genre recommendations for rock listeners to broaden their engagement with emerging artists.
